History

Definition: GMO stands for genetically modified organisms which are created by splicing DNA from another organism and putting them into another organism to advance the growth of an organism.

  • The Flavr Savr tomato was the first organisms to be accepted by the FDA and sold on the market.
  • Since the first approval of a GMO scientist has now engineered Corn, Cotton, and soybeans as there top products on the market. Which now takes up 80 to 95% of those crops are gentilic modified.
  • Nowadays people call GMO with these different slang terms biotech food, GMO, Genetic engineered food.

Process

How it works

  • Two organisms are crossbred to create a new brand.
  • They could be cross-pollinate.
  • Using a gene gun were the gene that is needed is injected into the organism that needs the gene.

How it Made

  • Identify a trait in an organism that appeals towards the consumer preference.
  • Isolate the trait and transfer it into a medium orgamism. One that will be the final stage product.
  • Methods of transferring this trait include selective breeding, or manual insertion of genes.
  • FDA defines GMO* through the process of genetic engineering ( meaning selective breeding does meet FDA standards for the tag).
  • Insertion of genetic material is commonly done by cutting and pasting genes into a plasmid using enzymes and using a “gene gun” to shoot DNA metal particles.
  • These growth of these mediums are carefully controlled in specialized growth chambers that are under constant moderation.
  • A lot of the production cost of GMO* has to deal with the growth stage of the mediums, along with the reproduction of these mediums for more product.
  • Automated machines are also used to keep track of plants and their optimal growth/spacing/nutritional requirements.
